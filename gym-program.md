# 2-Day Gym Program for BJJ (Brown Belt, 44yo)

## Context

44-year-old brown belt training BJJ 2-3x/week (gi and no-gi). Goal is a balanced 2-day gym program covering strength, injury prevention, and conditioning in 45-60 minute sessions. Full gym access, intermediate lifting experience, back-to-back days with BJJ possible.

## Programming Philosophy

At 44 and brown belt, the gym should **serve your mat time**, not compete with it. The priorities are:

1. **Stay healthy** — bulletproof the shoulders, neck, knees, and lower back that BJJ beats up
2. **Build useful strength** — hip drive, pulling power, grip endurance, rotational core
3. **Don't accumulate fatigue** — moderate volume, no training to failure, leave gas in the tank for BJJ

Rep ranges stay in the 3-5 range for main lifts (strength without excessive soreness) and 8-12 for accessories. Rest periods are 2-3 min on compounds, 60-90s on accessories.

---

## Weekly Schedule (Example)

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| **Gym A** | BJJ | Rest or light mobility | **Gym B** | BJJ | BJJ (optional) | Rest |

Adjust as needed. If gym and BJJ fall on consecutive days, put the gym day **before** a rest day when possible. If you double up (gym AM / BJJ PM), keep gym volume on the lighter end that day.

## Day A — Lower Body & Posterior Chain Focus

**Warmup (5 min)**
- Hip 90/90 switches — 5 each side
- World's greatest stretch — 3 each side
- Band pull-aparts — 15
- Goblet squat hold — 30s

| # | Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|---|----------|-------------|-------|
| A1 | **Trap bar deadlift** | 3 x 5 | Main strength lift. Easier on the lower back than conventional. If no trap bar, use conventional or sumo. RPE 7-8. |
| A2 | **Half-kneeling Pallof press** | 3 x 8/side | Anti-rotation core — directly transfers to guard retention and framing |
| B1 | **Bulgarian split squat** | 3 x 8/leg | Single-leg strength for passing, shooting, and knee stability. Hold dumbbells. |
| B2 | **Chest-supported dumbbell row** | 3 x 10 | Pulling volume without lower back fatigue. Builds the upper back for grips and posture. |
| C1 | **Nordic hamstring curl** (or hamstring slide) | 3 x 5-8 | Eccentric hamstring strength — major injury prevention for knees and hamstrings |
| C2 | **Face pulls** | 3 x 15 | Rear delt and external rotation health. Non-negotiable for BJJ shoulders. |
| D | **Farmer's carry** | 3 x 30-40m | Grip endurance, core stability, shoulder packing. Heavy as possible with good posture. |

**Cooldown:** Couch stretch 60s/side, pigeon stretch 60s/side

---

## Day B — Upper Body & Push/Pull Focus

**Warmup (5 min)**
- Band dislocates — 15
- Scapular push-ups — 10
- Dead hangs — 2 x 20s
- Turkish get-up — 1 each side (light KB)

| # | Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|---|----------|-------------|-------|
| A1 | **Weighted pull-ups** (or bodyweight) | 3 x 5 | Main upper body pull. Use a neutral grip to save the elbows. Add weight when bodyweight feels easy. |
| A2 | **Dumbbell bench press** | 3 x 6-8 | Pressing strength for framing and posting. Dumbbells are more shoulder-friendly than barbell. |
| B1 | **Single-arm dumbbell row** | 3 x 8/arm | Unilateral pulling — mimics collar/sleeve grips and arm drags |
| B2 | **Landmine press** | 3 x 8/arm | Shoulder-friendly pressing with a rotational element. Great for BJJ. |
| C1 | **Cable woodchop** (low to high) | 3 x 10/side | Rotational power for sweeps, hip escapes, and scrambles |
| C2 | **Prone Y-T-W raises** | 2 x 8 each | Scapular stability and shoulder health. Light weight, slow and controlled. |
| D | **Dead hang + scapular shrug** | 3 x 20-30s | Grip endurance, shoulder decompression, lat engagement. Great after a BJJ week. |

**Cooldown:** Doorway pec stretch 60s/side, thread-the-needle 5/side

## Progression

- **Main lifts (deadlift, pull-ups, bench):** Add weight every 2-3 weeks if all reps hit at RPE 7-8. Small jumps (2.5-5 lb).
- **Accessories:** Progress by adding 1-2 reps per set before increasing weight.
- **Deload:** Every 4-5 weeks, cut volume by 40% (2 sets instead of 3, lighter weight). Especially important during weeks with heavy BJJ training or competitions.

## Recovery Considerations

- **Sleep 7-8 hours** — this is the #1 performance variable at 44
- **Protein 1g/lb bodyweight** — supports recovery across both training modalities
- **If BJJ was intense the day before:** drop main lift to 2 working sets and keep accessories light
- **Competition prep:** drop gym to 1 day/week in the 2 weeks before a tournament, focus on mobility and light movement
